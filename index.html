Project Summary

Background & Motivation
The rapid growth of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) in industries such as logistics, emergency response, infrastructure inspection, and precision agriculture has created a demand for more efficient, precise, and adaptable flight control systems. While many commercial flight controllers are available, they often suffer from stability issues, high power consumption, and limited customization options, making them unsuitable for mission-critical and cutting-edge applications.

The Low SWaP (Size, Weight, and Power) Drone Flight Controller was developed to address these challenges, offering a lightweight, power-efficient, and highly modular flight control system.

IMU Math & Visualization

Acceleration data is a key indicator of IMU reliability. Since gravitational acceleration is a known constant (~9.81 m/s²), it can be used to validate sensor accuracy and drift. Visualizing the accelerometer's response over time provides a first look at IMU stability.

Upcoming additions will expand from acceleration to linear velocity (m/s) estimation by integrating acceleration data over time. This will allow motion tracking and help quantify noise across dynamic tests.

Future Testing – Linear Velocity

Linear velocity tests will involve dynamic motion across all axes. Each test will use timed movements over fixed distances to validate calculated velocity from integrated acceleration data.
