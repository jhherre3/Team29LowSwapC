Project Summary

Background & Motivation
The rapid growth of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) in industries such as logistics, emergency response, infrastructure inspection, and precision agriculture has created a demand for more efficient, precise, and adaptable flight control systems. While many commercial flight controllers are available, they often suffer from stability issues, high power consumption, and limited customization options, making them unsuitable for mission-critical and cutting-edge applications.

The Low SWaP (Size, Weight, and Power) Drone Flight Controller was developed to address these challenges, offering a lightweight, power-efficient, and highly modular flight control system.

System Architecture

  • Microcontroller (STM32F405): Executes real-time flight computations and supports CircuitPython/STM environments.
  • Honeywell I400 MEMS IMU: Provides precise inertial measurements; detachable for calibration.
  • USB-C Interface: Enables firmware updates and telemetry.
  • MicroSD Storage: Logs diagnostics and sensor data.
  • HMC5883L Magnetometer: Provides accurate heading data.
  • Flash Memory: Stores firmware and system data.
  • Power Management: Efficient consumption (≤5W), stable regulation.

Testing Results

The flight controller met key performance and design objectives. The PCB was successfully fabricated and assembled, integrating the STM32F405 microcontroller, Honeywell I400 IMU, and modular expansion capabilities. A custom sensor fusion algorithm was implemented, achieving stable and accurate UAV navigation data. Power efficiency targets were met, with operational consumption staying below 5W. The firmware framework was designed to support both CircuitPython and STM environments, making the system accessible to a range of developers. USB-C, microSD, and telemetry logging were successfully integrated. Efforts to integrate Betaflight are ongoing, with preliminary compatibility established.

FAQ

What is an IMU?
An Inertial Measurement Unit detects acceleration and rotation for navigation.

What does the barometer do?
Measures air pressure to estimate altitude.

What is SWaP-C?
Size, Weight, Power, and Cost — essential in aerospace systems.

What microcontroller is used?
We use the STM32F405 due to its performance and peripheral support.

What is sensor fusion?
Combining IMU, barometer, and GPS for accurate flight control data.

Is the firmware open-source?
Yes, it's optimized for embedded C/C++ development.

How does a drone fly?
Propellers provide lift, sensors provide data, and the flight controller keeps it balanced.

What makes this different?
It's small, lightweight, efficient, and designed for modular prototyping and testing.
